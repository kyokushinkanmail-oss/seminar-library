Positioning Theory — Angles, Inside and Outside

April 20, 2026 — Yusuke Usui & Hayato Chida

1. What is Positioning?

In kumite, where you stand is a question that comes before technique itself. The distance, the angle, and whether you are on the inside or outside of your opponent completely change which techniques are available to you.

Positioning is the element by which "the fight is decided before it starts." Once you control it consciously, the quality of your kumite changes at its core.

2. The Three Zones of Distance

DistanceCharacteristicsEffective techniques
Long rangeFront kick / jodan roundhouse kick reachesAll kicking techniques, leaping punches
Mid rangeStrikes land with a single stepPunches, knee strikes, chudan roundhouse kicks
Close rangeContact to half-step distanceKnee strikes, elbows (rules permitting), breaking balance

3. The Principle of Angle

Standing directly in front (0°) leaves you exposed to every attack your opponent can throw. By creating a 15°-30° offset, you neutralize roughly half of the incoming threats.

  • 30° to the outside — the angle for roundhouse and spinning back kicks
  • 15° to the inside — the angle for combination punches and knee strikes
  • The angle is determined by foot position — move your feet, not your upper body

4. Inside vs. Outside

Advantages of the inside

  • You enter on your opponent's lead-hand side, making it easier to shut down their lead hand
  • The punching distance is shorter, so combinations flow naturally
  • Easier to avoid the opponent's strong rear hand

Advantages of the outside

  • Easier to step into the opponent's blind side — your attacks are harder to see
  • You gain the angle for roundhouse and spinning back kicks
  • The opponent must rotate their body to counter — you own the tempo

5. Practical Drills

Drill 1: Side-step into a chudan punch

From a natural stance, step the lead foot diagonally forward to create an angle, then fire three consecutive chudan punches. Key point: "feet first, hands second." The feet must be set before the punch lands.

Drill 2: Circle outside into a roundhouse kick

Step around to the opponent's outside, and before they can turn to face you, throw a chudan roundhouse kick. Aim for an angle of at least 30° on the circle-step.

Drill 3: Step inside into a knee strike

While guarding with the lead hand, take a half-step inside the opponent, pin their lead hand, and deliver a knee strike. Because you are close, keep the chambering motion of the knee as small as possible.

6. Summary

  • Positioning is the most important element — decided before the technique
  • Simply being aware of a 15°-30° offset will transform your kumite
  • Remember: inside = punching distance, outside = kicking distance
  • In training, make "moving the feet first" the conscious priority
